Word 04: Preparing a Research Paper

In this module, you will learn some of Word’s unique features and commands for preparing academic and professional research papers. To begin, you learn how to create spreadsheet-like tables for grouping and presenting structured data. You then learn how to add reference sources such as books, magazines, and blogs that may be inserted into the document later as citations, footnotes, and endnotes. Lastly, you will polish a research paper by adding a table of contents to the beginning and a bibliography at the end of the document.

To follow along with the video lessons in this module, make sure
to download and unzip the Word 04 data files to your computer.

01. Working with Tables

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Insert a table of rows and columns for managing data

  • Add, delete, and move rows and columns

  • Apply a table style and customize the appearance of a table

  • Create a formula to sum a column of values

  • Sort a table into ascending and descending order

  • Modify common table properties

Creating and Formatting Tables (11:42m) — view on YouTube.

02. Citations & Footnotes

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Add references using the Source Manager

  • Insert and customize a citation with author, year, and page numbers

  • Insert and delete footnotes and endnotes

  • Customize the display of footnotes and endnotes

Adding Sources, Citations, Footnotes, and Endnotes (9:09m) — view on YouTube.

03. TOC & Bibliography

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Add Table and Figure captions to a document

  • Use styles to define headings in a table of contents

  • Add a table of contents to the beginning of a document

  • Choose a bibliographic style for references

  • Add a bibliography at the end of a document

Adding Captions, a Table of Contents, and Bibliography (9:10m) — view on YouTube.
